# Foreign Affairs Compares Iran and Ukraine Conflicts to Historical Wars

A new analysis published by **Foreign Affairs** draws striking parallels between current geopolitical conflicts and major Cold War-era wars, suggesting that similar patterns of conflict may lead to similar outcomes. The comparison frames Iran's situation as analogous to Vietnam, while Ukraine's conflict mirrors the Korean War, offering insights into how these modern disputes might ultimately be resolved.

The article, published in May 2026, examines the structural and strategic similarities between these conflicts across different historical periods. By drawing these historical comparisons, the analysis suggests that understanding how previous wars concluded may provide valuable context for predicting the trajectory of current international tensions.

## Historical Parallels and Strategic Similarities

The comparison between contemporary conflicts and Cold War-era wars highlights how certain patterns in international conflict tend to repeat themselves. The Vietnam War and Korean War, both pivotal moments in 20th-century geopolitics, established templates for how prolonged conflicts between superpowers and their allies can develop and eventually reach resolution.

According to **Foreign Affairs**, the structural similarities between these conflicts suggest that the ways in which Vietnam and Korea ultimately concluded may offer lessons for understanding how Iran and Ukraine situations could develop. These historical precedents provide a framework for analyzing how external powers become involved, how conflicts escalate or de-escalate, and what conditions typically lead to settlement or stalemate.

The analysis underscores how geopolitical conflicts often follow recognizable patterns, even when separated by decades and different regional contexts. The involvement of major powers, the commitment of resources, and the ideological dimensions of these conflicts create comparable dynamics that shape their progression and eventual outcomes.
