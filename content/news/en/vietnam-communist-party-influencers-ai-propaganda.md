# Vietnam's Communist Party Plans Influencers and AI for Propaganda Overhaul

Vietnam's ruling Communist Party is set to modernize its propaganda efforts by incorporating social media influencers and artificial intelligence, as revealed in internal documents reported on May 8, 2026. This initiative, aimed at enhancing the effectiveness of state messaging in a digital age, reflects the party's ongoing efforts to maintain ideological control amid rapid technological advancements and growing online engagement. The move comes as Vietnam, a one-party communist state, seeks to adapt its communication strategies to reach younger audiences and counter external influences.

## Background on Vietnam's Propaganda Landscape

Vietnam's Communist Party has long relied on traditional media and state-controlled outlets to disseminate its ideology and shape public opinion. As a socialist republic governed by the Communist Party of Vietnam since 1975, the country maintains strict oversight of information flow, with propaganda playing a central role in reinforcing party loyalty and national unity. Documents obtained and analyzed by **Reuters** indicate that the party now recognizes the limitations of conventional methods in an era dominated by social media platforms and digital tools.

The plan involves drafting in social media influencers—popular figures with large online followings—to subtly promote party narratives. This approach mirrors global trends where governments and organizations leverage influencers for outreach, but in Vietnam's context, it underscores a strategic pivot to make propaganda more relatable and less overt. Artificial intelligence enters the picture as a tool for content creation, audience analysis, and targeted dissemination, potentially automating the generation of messages tailored to specific demographics.

While specifics on implementation timelines or budgets remain undisclosed in the available documents, this revamp signals the party's intent to blend traditional Marxist-Leninist principles with contemporary tech innovations. Vietnam's media environment is already tightly regulated; for instance, internet access is widespread, but content is monitored through laws like the 2018 Cybersecurity Law, which requires platforms to remove material deemed harmful to national security. The integration of AI could amplify these controls, enabling faster detection and response to dissenting voices online.

This development is not isolated. Vietnam has been investing in digital infrastructure, with internet penetration exceeding 70% of the population as of recent public reports. The government's "Make in Vietnam" initiative promotes local tech development, including AI applications in various sectors. By applying these to propaganda, the party aims to "spruce up" its efforts, making them more engaging for a tech-savvy youth demographic that spends significant time on platforms like Facebook, TikTok, and Zalo.

## What's Next for Vietnam's Digital Propaganda Strategy

Looking ahead, the Communist Party's adoption of influencers and AI is likely to unfold gradually, integrated into existing propaganda departments. Internal documents suggest pilot programs could test these tools in urban areas with high social media usage, such as Ho Chi Minh City and Hanoi. Success metrics might include increased engagement rates or improved public sentiment toward party policies, though exact evaluation methods are not detailed.

Challenges abound. Recruiting credible influencers without alienating audiences could prove tricky, given Vietnam's history of crackdowns on online dissent. AI implementation will require technical expertise, potentially involving partnerships with domestic tech firms. Internationally, this could draw scrutiny from human rights groups concerned about information control in authoritarian contexts.
