# Vietnam's Communist Party Plans Influencers and AI for Propaganda Boost

Vietnam's ruling Communist Party is set to overhaul its longstanding propaganda strategies by integrating social media influencers and artificial intelligence tools, according to internal documents reviewed by [Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/sustainability/society-equity/communist-run-vietnam-eyes-influencers-ai-spruce-up-propaganda-documents-show-2026-05-08/). This initiative, aimed at enhancing the party's messaging in a digital age, reflects broader efforts to adapt to evolving communication landscapes in the Southeast Asian nation. The plans come as Vietnam, a one-party state governed by the Communist Party since 1975, seeks to maintain ideological control amid rapid internet penetration and youth engagement on platforms like Facebook and TikTok. While specifics on timelines or implementation remain unclear from the available information, the move underscores the party's recognition of modern tools' power in shaping public opinion.

## Background on Vietnam's Propaganda Evolution

Vietnam's propaganda apparatus has long been a cornerstone of the Communist Party's governance, rooted in the principles of Marxism-Leninism and Ho Chi Minh Thought. Historically, state-controlled media outlets such as the Vietnam News Agency and newspapers like Nhan Dan have disseminated official narratives on everything from economic achievements to national unity. These efforts have been essential in a country where freedom of speech is limited, and the government tightly regulates information to align with party directives.

The introduction of social media influencers and AI marks a significant departure from traditional methods. Social media influencers, often young creators with large followings, have become cultural forces in Vietnam, where internet users number over 70 million—more than two-thirds of the population. Platforms like YouTube, Instagram, and TikTok are popular among the youth, who increasingly turn to online personalities for lifestyle, travel, and entertainment content. By drafting in influencers, the party aims to leverage this grassroots appeal to make propaganda more relatable and less overtly state-driven.

Artificial intelligence, meanwhile, represents a technological leap. AI tools could automate content creation, personalize messaging, or analyze public sentiment through data mining. In communist states like China, which Vietnam often emulates, AI has been used for surveillance and tailored propaganda, such as chatbots promoting party policies. While the documents do not detail exact applications, it's widely known that Vietnam has been investing in digital infrastructure, including 5G networks and data centers, to support such advancements. This aligns with the party's 13th National Congress resolutions, which emphasize science and technology for national development.

It's important to note that what is known comes solely from the Reuters-reported documents; broader details on budget, specific influencers involved, or AI vendors are not public. Speculation about aggressive censorship or deepfake usage would be unfounded without further evidence, though Vietnam's existing cyber laws already penalize "anti-state" online content.

## What's Next for Vietnam's Digital Strategy

The Communist Party's plans signal a proactive stance in an era where information warfare is global. Implementation might begin with pilot programs, perhaps targeting urban youth in Hanoi or Ho Chi Minh City, where social media adoption is highest. Training sessions for party cadres on influencer collaboration or AI ethics could follow, drawing from international models while adhering to Vietnam's socialist orientation.

Challenges abound: Influencers risk losing authenticity if perceived as state puppets, and AI raises privacy concerns in a nation already criticized for internet controls. The documents suggest a "spruce up" rather than overhaul, implying evolution over revolution.
