# Vietnam's $5 Billion Phu Quoc Island Faces Time Crunch Before APEC

Vietnam's ambitious **$5 billion development project** on [Phu Quoc island](https://go2-vietnam.com/city/phu-quoc/) is racing against the clock, with only 18 months remaining to complete infrastructure ahead of a major APEC summit. According to the **South China Morning Post**, the island is struggling with critical shortages of workers, diesel fuel, and hotel rooms—raising serious questions about whether the "build-fast" model can deliver on schedule.

The project represents Vietnam's largest infrastructure bet in recent years, positioning Phu Quoc as a showcase for the nation's economic ambitions. However, the ambitious timeline is colliding with real-world constraints that threaten to derail completion.

## The Challenge Ahead

With less than 18 months to go, Phu Quoc faces a perfect storm of logistical obstacles. The island is experiencing acute shortages across multiple critical areas: a lack of available workers to sustain construction pace, insufficient diesel supplies to power machinery and transport, and inadequate hotel capacity to accommodate the influx of visitors expected for the APEC event.

These aren't minor hiccups—they strike at the heart of what makes large-scale infrastructure projects feasible. Without sufficient labor, fuel, and hospitality infrastructure, the island cannot simultaneously complete construction while preparing to host a major international summit.

The situation underscores the tension between Vietnam's rapid development model and the practical realities of executing megaprojects on a remote island with limited existing capacity. Phu Quoc, while increasingly developed as a tourism destination, still lacks the deep supply chains and workforce reserves that mainland industrial hubs enjoy.

## What This Means for Visitors

For travelers planning trips to [Phu Quoc](https://go2-vietnam.com/city/phu-quoc/), the development crunch could create both challenges and opportunities. In the short term, ongoing construction may impact the visitor experience, with potential disruptions to roads, beaches, and services as work accelerates. Accommodation availability could become tighter as the island prioritizes APEC-related facilities.

However, the massive investment also signals significant improvements ahead. New hotels, improved transportation infrastructure, and enhanced facilities are being built specifically to elevate Phu Quoc's status as a premium destination. Once completed, these upgrades should substantially improve the experience for future visitors.

Travelers should monitor local news and check with accommodations before booking, as construction timelines may affect specific areas of the island. The period immediately before and after the APEC summit may also see higher prices and reduced availability.

## What's Next

Vietnam faces a critical decision point. The government must either secure additional resources—more workers, fuel supplies, and construction capacity—or adjust expectations about what can realistically be completed by the APEC deadline. Some projects may need to be prioritized over others, or timelines may require extension.

The success or failure of Phu Quoc's development will have implications beyond the island itself. It will serve as a test case for Vietnam's ability to execute large-scale infrastructure projects under tight deadlines—a capability that investors and international partners closely watch.

For now, Phu Quoc remains a construction site with enormous potential but mounting pressure. Whether Vietnam's build-fast model can overcome the current bottlenecks will become clear in the coming months.
