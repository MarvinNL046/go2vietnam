# AI Backlash Grows as Gen Z Rejects Technology, Drawing Vietnam War Parallels

A significant backlash against artificial intelligence is gaining momentum, with **Generation Z** leading vocal opposition to the technology at major public events. According to **Marcus on AI | Substack**, multiple commencement speakers—including former **Google CEO Eric Schmidt**—were booed in recent days simply for mentioning artificial intelligence, signaling a dramatic shift in public sentiment toward the industry.

The growing resistance has prompted tech commentators to draw historical parallels. **Jason Calacanis** of All In compared the movement to the Vietnam War era, describing AI as "Gen Z's Vietnam," noting that the generation wants nothing to do with the technology and is actively opposing it. However, some analysts believe the analogy runs deeper than a simple generational rejection, touching on fundamental questions about massive investments with questionable returns.

## The Vietnam War Parallel and Tech Industry Spending

The comparison to Vietnam carries weight when examining the financial scale of AI development. The Vietnam War cost the United States approximately **a trillion dollars in today's money**, drained resources, and continued for years despite evidence it wasn't achieving its stated objectives. Critics now question whether the multitrillion-dollar investment in artificial intelligence—burning money at unprecedented rates while still struggling with hallucinations, unreliability, and alignment issues—represents a similar arrogance-fueled mistake.

The lack of return on investment in most AI pilot studies, combined with persistent reliability problems, has fueled skepticism about whether the massive spending is justified. This economic argument, combined with generational concerns about the technology's impact, appears to be driving the current backlash.

## Policy Shifts and Political Realignment

In a significant development, political leadership appears to be responding to the growing opposition. According to the source, **President Trump** is now actively considering AI preflight checks similar to FDA regulatory frameworks—a dramatic reversal from his aggressively pro-AI industry policies in 2025. This shift aligns with predictions made earlier in 2026 that Trump would begin distancing himself from industry-friendly AI policies by year's end.

The proposed regulatory approach would govern AI applications similarly to pharmaceutical approval processes, with authorities evaluating and regulating the release of new major applications based on evidence of safety. This represents a substantial policy pivot that could reshape how the technology is developed and deployed.

## What This Means for the Future

The convergence of generational opposition and political recalibration suggests the AI industry may face increased regulatory scrutiny and public resistance. While some analysts believe the backlash alone cannot stop AI development, the combination of vocal opposition and policy shifts could meaningfully alter the trajectory of the technology's rollout and implementation.

The situation remains fluid, with observers noting that if the backlash maintains sufficient momentum, political leaders may move even further away from anti-regulatory stances. The outcome could determine whether AI development proceeds along its current path or is redirected toward what advocates describe as "a better place"—one with stronger safeguards and more transparent governance.

As these dynamics continue to evolve, the tech industry faces an unprecedented challenge: balancing innovation with public accountability in an era of heightened skepticism about technological progress.
