The **USS Gerald R. Ford**, the world's largest aircraft carrier, returned to Naval Station Norfolk in Virginia on Saturday, May 16, 2026, after completing an **11-month deployment**—the longest for a U.S. aircraft carrier since the Vietnam War. The historic homecoming marked the end of a mission that saw the carrier and its accompanying destroyers support American military operations in Iran and participate in the capture of Venezuelan president Nicolás Maduro.

Approximately **5,000 sailors** aboard the carrier and two accompanying destroyers—the **USS Bainbridge** and **USS Mahan**—reunited with their families for the first time since June 2025. The arrival was marked by the presence of U.S. Defense Secretary **Pete Hegseth**, who commended the crews for their service and achievement.

## A Record-Breaking Deployment

The Ford's **326 days at sea** represent the most time deployed for any aircraft carrier in the past 50 years, according to reporting from [Fortune](https://fortune.com/2026/05/16/uss-gerald-ford-aircraft-carrier-return-11-month-deployment-iran-war-maduro-raid/). The deployment broke the record for the longest post-Vietnam War carrier mission, underscoring the intensity and duration of recent global military operations.

Beyond the extended timeline, the crew faced significant challenges during their mission. A noncombat-related fire aboard the carrier left hundreds of sailors without sleeping quarters and necessitated lengthy repairs at the Greek island of Crete. Despite these obstacles, the crew maintained operational readiness throughout the deployment.

## Recognition and Awards

In recognition of their exceptional service during the Iran war operations, the **USS Gerald R. Ford** and its accompanying ships were awarded the prestigious **Presidential Unit Citation**. This is the highest award a military unit can receive and is typically reserved for significant achievement in combat. The citation specifically recognized the crew's "outstanding performance in action" against "a determined enemy."

Defense Secretary Hegseth addressed the crews directly, telling them: "You didn't just accomplish a mission, you made history. You made a nation proud." His remarks underscored the significance of the deployment and the crew's contributions to U.S. military objectives.

## What This Means for Global Operations

The Ford's record-breaking deployment reflects the expanded role of U.S. naval forces in maintaining global security and responding to emerging threats. The carrier's involvement in both the Iran conflict and operations related to Venezuelan political developments demonstrates the versatility of modern carrier strike groups in addressing multiple simultaneous challenges across different regions.

The extended deployment also highlights the strain placed on naval personnel and equipment during prolonged missions. The fire incident aboard the Ford, while noncombat-related, illustrated the challenges of maintaining a complex warship during months-long operations far from home ports.

## What's Next

With the Ford now back at its home port in Norfolk, the carrier and its crew will enter a maintenance and readiness phase. The ship's return allows for repairs, crew rest, and preparation for future deployments. The successful completion of this historic mission establishes a new benchmark for carrier operations and demonstrates the capability of the U.S. Navy to sustain extended global operations.

The homecoming also provides relief to the families of the 5,000 sailors who have been separated from their loved ones for nearly a year. The return of the world's most advanced warship to U.S. waters marks the conclusion of one of the most significant naval deployments in recent history.
