# Australian Travel to Vietnam Surges to 10-Year High

The number of Australians travelling to Vietnam has more than doubled over the past decade, transforming the country into one of Southeast Asia's most popular short-haul destinations for Australian holidaymakers. According to [The Guardian](https://www.theguardian.com/travel/2026/may/25/tourism-australian-travel-to-vietnam-south-east-asia-10-year-high), this surge reflects a significant shift in travel preferences driven by affordability, proximity, and strong value for money in a period of global economic uncertainty.

## Vietnam's Rise in Australian Travel Rankings

Australian Bureau of Statistics data reveals the dramatic shift in regional travel patterns. A decade ago, Vietnam ranked as the fifth most common Southeast Asian destination for short-term overseas travel from Australia. By 2024, it had climbed to third place, overtaking both Singapore and Malaysia. The raw numbers tell an even more striking story: the total number of Australian residents returning from short trips to Vietnam has more than doubled since 2016, rising from 246,000 to 528,000 in 2024.

This growth extends beyond official statistics. **Jetstar**, a major carrier servicing the Australia-Vietnam route, has increased passenger numbers by more than 30% since 2023 and now operates 15% more flights on these routes. Travel aggregator Expedia has also documented consistent year-on-year growth in accommodation searches for Vietnamese cities including [Hanoi](https://go2-vietnam.com/city/hanoi/), [Ho Chi Minh City](https://go2-vietnam.com/city/ho-chi-minh-city/), and [Phu Quoc](https://go2-vietnam.com/city/phu-quoc/) between 2025 and 2026.

## What's Driving the Boom

Dr Truc Le, a senior lecturer in marketing and tourism at Griffith University, attributes the surge to a "very strong alignment" between what Australian travellers want and what Vietnam offers. Several factors converge to explain the trend.

**Affordability and value** remain paramount. With cost-of-living pressures affecting Australian households, long-haul trips to iconic destinations like Paris or Venice have become less viable. Vietnam delivers what Le describes as "premium experiences at mid-range prices"—a critical appeal for budget-conscious travellers.

The **Australian dollar's strength** against the Vietnamese dong has amplified this advantage. Indexed to 2025 exchange rates, the Australian currency has experienced particular growth against the dong compared with the US dollar and euro, extending purchasing power further.

**Proximity** also matters. As global uncertainty persists, shorter journeys closer to home appeal more than ever. Vietnam's location makes it an accessible alternative to more distant destinations.

Real travellers confirm these advantages. **Mandy Lan**, a 25-year-old event coordinator, spent just under $1,500 on a two-week trip at the end of 2025 (excluding flights), dining at Michelin-starred restaurants and commissioning five custom-made garments for $250 through Vietnam's renowned tailoring industry. Lifestyle content creator **Georgia Quinn** budgeted $6,000 for a two-week holiday with her partner, flights included—a cost structure that would be unthinkable in many Western destinations.

## Visible Changes on the Ground

The influx is reshaping Vietnam's tourism landscape. Quinn recently noted significant new developments in the coastal city of [Da Nang](https://go2-vietnam.com/city/da-nang/), describing it as becoming "like the Gold Coast of Vietnam." She also observed a marked increase in Australian visitors during her latest trip, a phenomenon she attributes to word-of-mouth recommendations spreading across her social networks.

## What This Means for Visitors

The surge in Australian tourism to Vietnam reflects broader travel trends favouring value, proximity, and authentic experiences over expensive long-haul trips. For prospective visitors, this growth signals both opportunity and potential crowding in popular areas. Travellers planning trips should consider visiting during shoulder seasons or exploring lesser-known regions to avoid peak tourist periods while still enjoying Vietnam's renowned hospitality and affordability.

The trend shows no signs of slowing, with airlines expanding capacity and accommodation providers responding to sustained demand across major cities and beach destinations.
