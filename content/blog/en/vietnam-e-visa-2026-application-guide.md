## 1. What Exactly Is a Vietnam E-Visa?

The **Vietnam e-visa** is an electronic travel authorization issued by the Vietnamese Immigration Department that allows you to enter Vietnam for tourism, business, or temporary visits. Unlike traditional visas stamped in your passport at an embassy, the e-visa exists only digitally—you print it and present it at immigration upon arrival. It's valid for 90 days from the date of issue, permits a single entry, and costs a fraction of what you'd pay for a paper visa through an embassy.

The e-visa system launched in February 2017 to streamline tourism and reduce bureaucratic friction. Since then, it has become the go-to option for independent travelers, business visitors, and anyone who wants to skip the embassy queue. When we researched visa trends across Southeast Asia, Vietnam's e-visa emerged as one of the most straightforward and affordable options available to international travelers.

### Who Can Apply for a Vietnam E-Visa?

The e-visa is available to citizens of 46 countries and territories, including the United States, United Kingdom, Canada, Australia, most European nations, and many others. However, citizens of a few countries—including Vietnam, China, and some others—must apply through traditional embassy channels or visa-on-arrival services.

To check if your nationality is eligible, visit the official [Vietnam Immigration Department portal at evisa.gov.vn](https://evisa.gov.vn/) before you start your application. The eligibility list is updated regularly, and it takes 30 seconds to verify. If your country isn't listed, you'll need to explore [alternative visa options](https://go2-vietnam.com/visa/) or contact a Vietnamese embassy directly.

### How Long Is a Vietnam E-Visa Valid?

The e-visa is valid for **90 days from the date of issue**, not from your arrival date. This means if you apply on June 1st and receive approval on June 2nd, your visa expires on September 1st, regardless of when you actually enter Vietnam. Plan your application timing carefully—apply no more than 90 days before your intended arrival to avoid the visa expiring before you use it.

The visa permits **single entry only**, meaning once you leave Vietnam, the visa becomes invalid. If you plan to leave Vietnam and return during your trip, you'll need to apply for a new e-visa or arrange a multiple-entry visa through an embassy before departure.

## 2. Vietnam E-Visa Costs & Processing Options in 2026

The cost of a Vietnam e-visa depends on how quickly you need it processed. The official Vietnam Immigration Department charges three tiers: standard, urgent, and super urgent. All prices are in USD, paid directly through the official portal.

### Standard Processing (1 Business Day)

**Cost: USD 25**

Standard processing takes approximately 1 business day (24 hours) from the time you submit your application. This is the most economical option and works perfectly if you're planning your trip more than a week in advance. "Business day" means Monday through Friday, excluding Vietnamese public holidays—so if you apply on a Friday afternoon, expect approval by Monday or Tuesday.

During our team's testing of the application portal in early 2026, we submitted a standard application on a Wednesday morning and received approval by Thursday evening, well within the 24-hour window. The approved e-visa arrives via email as a PDF document that you download and print.

### Urgent Processing (4 Hours)

**Cost: USD 8–USD 17**

Urgent processing guarantees approval within 4 hours of submission. This option is ideal if you're leaving for Vietnam within a day or two and haven't yet applied. The exact cost within the USD 8–USD 17 range depends on the processing service and any additional fees, so verify the final amount before payment.

### Super Urgent Processing (2 Hours)

**Cost: Varies (typically USD 17+)**

If you need your e-visa approved within 2 hours, super urgent processing is available, though at a premium cost. This option is rarely necessary for leisure travelers but can be a lifesaver if you've booked a [last-minute](https://booking.tpo.lv/pDNjHJA1?subid=blog) flight or had a passport issue resolved unexpectedly.

> **Did You Know?** Vietnam's e-visa system processes over 500,000 applications annually, making it one of Southeast Asia's most-used digital visa platforms.

> *Source: [Vietnam Immigration Department](https://evisa.gov.vn/)*

### Payment Methods

The official portal accepts payment via international credit and debit cards (Visa, Mastercard, American Express). Payment is processed immediately, and your application enters the queue right away. Some third-party visa services offer alternative payment methods like bank transfer or PayPal, but always verify you're using the official government portal to avoid scams.

## 3. Step-by-Step Application Process

Applying for a Vietnam e-visa online is straightforward and takes about 15 minutes. Here's exactly what you'll do:

### Step 1: Visit the Official Portal

Navigate to [evisa.gov.vn](https://evisa.gov.vn/) or [thithucdientu.gov.vn](https://thithucdientu.gov.vn/). Both are official government portals operated by Vietnam's Immigration Department. The interface is available in English, Vietnamese, and several other languages. Select your language and click "Apply for E-Visa" or the equivalent button.

### Step 2: Fill in Your Personal Information

You'll be asked to provide:
- **Full name** (as it appears in your passport)
- **Date of birth**
- **Nationality**
- **Passport number**
- **Passport expiry date** (must be at least 6 months from your intended arrival date)
- **Email address** (where your approved e-visa will be sent)
- **Intended date of entry** to Vietnam
- **Port of entry** (airport, seaport, or land border where you'll arrive)

Double-check every field for accuracy. Any mismatch between your application and your passport will cause problems at immigration. During our testing, we found that even a single letter difference in spelling caused delays.

### Step 3: Upload Your Passport Photo

You'll need a digital passport photo meeting these specifications:
- **Size:** 4×6 cm (1.5×2.4 inches)
- **Format:** JPG or PNG
- **Background:** White or light-colored
- **Face visibility:** Clear, frontal view, eyes open
- **File size:** Under 500 KB

If you don't have a digital photo, you can take one with your smartphone against a white wall—it doesn't need to be professionally taken, just clear and properly sized. Many online tools can resize and crop photos to passport specifications for free.

### Step 4: Upload Your Passport Bio Page

Scan or photograph the biographical page of your passport (the page with your photo, name, and passport number). The image should be clear and readable. JPG or PNG format works fine.

### Step 5: Select Processing Speed & Pay

Choose your processing option (standard, urgent, or super urgent) and proceed to payment. Enter your credit card details and confirm. Payment is processed immediately, and you'll receive a confirmation email with your application reference number.

### Step 6: Wait for Approval

Check your email (including spam folder) for your approved e-visa. It will arrive as a PDF attachment. Download it, open it, and print it on standard white paper. You don't need color printing—black and white is fine.

## 4. Required Documents & Eligibility Checklist

Before you start your application, gather these documents and verify your eligibility:

**Essential Documents:**
- **Valid passport** with at least 6 months validity remaining (from your intended arrival date)
- **Digital passport photo** (4×6 cm, white background, clear face)
- **Scanned passport bio page** (the page with your photo and personal details)
- **Email address** (for receiving your approved e-visa)
- **Credit or debit card** (Visa, Mastercard, or American Express for payment)

**Eligibility Requirements:**
- Your nationality must be one of the 46+ eligible countries
- Your passport must be valid for at least 6 months beyond your intended arrival
- You must not have a criminal record or immigration violations (the system will flag certain cases)
- You must be applying for tourism, business, or temporary visit purposes (not work or long-term residence)

If you're unsure about your eligibility or have special circumstances (previous visa denials, criminal history, etc.), contact the Vietnamese Immigration Department directly before applying. Submitting a questionable application wastes your money and time.

## 5. Where You Can Enter Vietnam with an E-Visa

Your e-visa is valid at 30+ official ports of entry across Vietnam, including major airports, seaports, and land borders. When you apply, you must specify which port you'll use—you cannot change it after approval.

### Major Airports (Most Popular)

- **Noi Bai International Airport** (Hanoi) – Vietnam's largest airport, serving most international flights from Europe and North America
- **Tan Son Nhat International Airport** (Ho Chi Minh City) – The busiest airport, with connections to most major Asian hubs
- **Da Nang International Airport** – Central Vietnam's main hub, increasingly popular for direct flights from Asia

All three airports have dedicated immigration counters for e-visa holders. Processing is usually quick—under 5 minutes if your documents are in order. When we traveled through [Hanoi](https://go2-vietnam.com/city/hanoi/) in early 2026, the e-visa line moved faster than traditional visa lines.

### Seaports

If you're arriving by cruise ship, e-visa is accepted at major seaports including:
- **Hai Phong Port** (near [Ha Long Bay](https://go2-vietnam.com/city/ha-long/))
- **Ho Chi Minh City Port** (Saigon River)
- **Da Nang Port**

### Land Borders

E-visa is accepted at most major land border crossings, including:
- **Mong Cai** (China border, northeast)
- **Lao Cai** (China border, northwest)
- **Lao Bao** (Laos border, central)
- **Moc Bai** (Cambodia border, south)

Land border crossings can be slower than airports, and some smaller borders may have less [experience](https://getyourguide.tpo.lv/GuAFfGGK?subid=blog) with e-visas. Always double-check that your specific border crossing accepts e-visas before you arrive.

## 6. Common Mistakes to Avoid

During our research and testing of the e-visa system, we identified several mistakes that cause rejections or delays:

**Passport Validity Issues**

The most common rejection reason is a passport that expires within 6 months of your arrival date. Vietnam's immigration rules are strict about this—if your passport expires on December 15th and you're arriving on July 1st, you're fine. But if it expires on December 1st, your application will be rejected. Check your passport expiry date before applying.

**Photo Quality Problems**

Blurry photos, wrong background colors, or incorrect sizing cause rejections. Use a clear, recent photo with a white or very light background. If you're unsure, use an online passport photo tool to ensure specifications are met.

**Mismatched Information**

Your application must match your passport exactly—same spelling of your name, same date of birth, same passport number. Even a typo will cause problems at immigration. Proofread every field twice before submitting.

**Wrong Port of Entry**

You must specify where you'll enter Vietnam, and you cannot change it after approval. If your plans change and you need to enter at a different port, you'll need to apply for a new e-visa. Plan your arrival point carefully.

**Applying Too Early or Too Late**

Remember, the e-visa is valid for 90 days from issue, not from arrival. If you apply more than 90 days before your trip, your visa will expire before you use it. Apply no more than 60–70 days in advance to be safe.

**Using Unofficial Websites**

Dozens of third-party visa services advertise Vietnam e-visas online. While some are legitimate agents, many are scams. Always apply through the official government portal ([evisa.gov.vn](https://evisa.gov.vn/) or [thithucdientu.gov.vn](https://thithucdientu.gov.vn/)) to guarantee legitimacy and avoid overpaying.

## 7. What to Do When Your E-Visa Arrives

Once your e-visa is approved, you'll receive an email with a PDF attachment. Here's how to handle it:

### Download & Print Your E-Visa

Open the PDF and print it on standard white paper. You need one physical copy—color printing isn't necessary, black and white works fine. The document is typically one page and includes a QR code, your photo, and entry details.

### Keep a Digital Backup

Save the PDF to your phone, cloud storage, or email so you have a backup if you lose the printed copy. Many travelers take a photo of the printed e-visa as well, just in case.

### Present It at Immigration

When you arrive at your port of entry in Vietnam, go to the immigration counter and present your printed e-visa along with your passport. The officer will scan the QR code, verify your information, and stamp your passport. The entire process takes 2–5 minutes.

### What Happens If You Lose It?

If you lose your printed e-visa before arrival, you can still enter Vietnam using the digital copy on your phone or by reprinting it. However, it's easier to have the physical copy ready. If you lose it after arrival, it doesn't matter—your passport has been stamped.

## 10. Frequently Asked Questions

### Can I extend my Vietnam e-visa?

No, the e-visa cannot be extended. It's valid for exactly 90 days from the date of issue. If you need to stay longer, you must apply for a new e-visa or arrange a longer-term visa before your current one expires. Many travelers apply for a new e-visa while still in Vietnam if they decide to extend their trip.

### What if my e-visa application is rejected?

Rejections are rare but happen if your passport is invalid, your photo doesn't meet specifications, or your nationality isn't eligible. If rejected, you'll receive an email explaining the reason. You can reapply immediately after fixing the issue. There's no penalty for reapplying.

### Can I use my e-visa to work in Vietnam?

No, the e-visa is strictly for tourism, business meetings, and temporary visits. If you plan to work in Vietnam, you need a work permit and separate visa. The e-visa will be rejected at immigration if you're caught working.

### Do I need travel insurance with an e-visa?

The e-visa doesn't require travel insurance, but we strongly recommend getting [travel insurance for Vietnam](https://go2-vietnam.com/travel-insurance/) anyway. It covers medical emergencies, flight cancellations, and lost baggage—essential protection for any international trip.

### Can I apply for an e-visa if I'm already in Vietnam?

No, the e-visa is only for people outside Vietnam. If you're already in the country and need to extend your stay, you must apply for a visa extension through the Immigration Department office in person.

### What's the difference between evisa.gov.vn and thithucdientu.gov.vn?

Both are official government portals operated by Vietnam's Immigration Department. In November 2024, the system migrated to new domains. Both sites function identically—use whichever one you find first.

### Do I need a return ticket to apply for an e-visa?

No, the e-visa application doesn't ask for proof of a return ticket. However, immigration officers may ask to see onward travel plans when you arrive, so it's wise to have a return flight booked or at least a plan for leaving Vietnam.

### How much should I budget for a Vietnam trip?
