## 1. What Is a Vietnam E-Visa and Who Needs One?

The **Vietnam e-visa** is an electronic travel authorization issued by the Vietnam Immigration Department that allows foreign nationals to enter and stay in Vietnam for tourism, business, or transit purposes. Unlike traditional visas requiring a physical stamp in your passport, the e-visa exists as a digital document linked to your passport number. When you arrive at an eligible entry point, immigration officials scan your passport and verify your e-visa status in their system.

Most international visitors to Vietnam require a visa, with a few exceptions. Citizens of certain ASEAN countries and a handful of other nations enjoy visa-free entry for short stays, but for most travelers, the e-visa is the most convenient and cost-effective option. Our team has researched the application process extensively, and the online system has become increasingly user-friendly since its introduction.

### Who Is Eligible for an E-Visa?

Citizens of over 190 countries are eligible to apply for a Vietnam e-visa, including the United States, United Kingdom, Canada, Australia, European Union member states, and most other nations. The official Vietnam e-visa portal maintains a complete list of eligible nationalities—it's worth checking before you begin your application. A few countries have restrictions or require different visa types, so verification is essential.

### Single Entry vs. Multiple Entry

The Vietnam e-visa comes in two variants. The **single-entry e-visa** allows you to enter Vietnam once and stay for up to 90 days from your entry date. If you plan to leave and re-enter Vietnam during your trip—for example, to visit [Cambodia or Thailand](https://go2-vietnam.com/region/north-vietnam/)—you'll need a **multiple-entry e-visa**, which permits unlimited entries and exits over the same 90-day validity period. The multiple-entry option costs roughly double but offers significantly more flexibility for regional travel.

> **Did You Know?** The Vietnam e-visa system processes over 10,000 applications daily, making it one of Southeast Asia's busiest visa portals.

## 2. Understanding E-Visa Costs and Payment Options

When budgeting for your Vietnam trip, the e-visa fee is a straightforward expense. The official government fee for a **single-entry e-visa is USD 25**, while a **multiple-entry e-visa costs USD 50**. These are the base fees charged by the Vietnam Immigration Department on the official portal.

However, many travelers use third-party visa service providers, which add processing fees on top of the government charge. During our research, we found that total costs through service providers typically range from USD 30–40 for standard processing and USD 50–80 for expedited services. While these providers charge more, they offer convenience, customer support, and faster turnaround times—trade-offs worth considering if you're short on time or uncomfortable navigating the official portal in Vietnamese.

### Official Portal vs. Service Providers

The most economical route is applying directly through the official Vietnam e-visa portal at [https://evisa.gov.vn/](https://evisa.gov.vn/), where you pay only the government fee. The process is straightforward if you're comfortable with English-language instructions and have all required documents ready. Payment is accepted via international credit cards, debit cards, and some digital payment methods.

Third-party service providers handle the application on your behalf, reducing the risk of errors and offering customer support in multiple languages. If you're applying during peak travel season or have concerns about meeting deadlines, these services provide peace of mind—though at a premium cost. When choosing a provider, verify they're reputable and check recent traveler reviews.

## 3. Step-by-Step Application Process on the Official Portal

Applying for a Vietnam e-visa through the official government portal is a straightforward process that takes 10–15 minutes if you have all documents prepared. Here's exactly what to expect:

### Gathering Your Required Documents

Before you start, ensure you have these items ready:

- **Valid passport** with at least six months validity remaining from your intended entry date
- **Digital passport photo** (4×6 cm, white background, taken within the last six months)
- **Scanned passport information page** (the page with your photo and personal details)
- Your intended **entry and exit dates**
- Details of your **entry port** (airport, seaport, or land border)

The passport photo is the most common source of application rejections. It must be a clear, recent color photo with your face centered, neutral expression, and no glasses or head coverings (unless for religious reasons). Many travelers use smartphone photos taken against a white wall—these work fine as long as the lighting is even and your face is clearly visible.

### Completing the Online Application

Visit the official portal at [https://evisa.gov.vn/](https://evisa.gov.vn/) and select your nationality from the dropdown menu. The form asks for:

- Full name (as it appears in your passport)
- Date of birth
- Passport number and issue/expiry dates
- Email address
- Phone number
- Intended entry and exit dates
- Entry port (select from the official list)
- Visa type (single or multiple entry)

Take your time filling out each field—errors can delay processing or result in rejection. Double-check your passport number and dates especially, as these must match your physical passport exactly. Once submitted, you'll receive a confirmation email with an application reference number.

### Payment and Submission

After completing the form, you'll be directed to the payment page. The system accepts international credit and debit cards. Enter your card details and confirm the payment. You'll receive an immediate payment confirmation and a reference number. Save this number—you'll use it to track your application status.

## 4. Processing Times: Standard vs. Expedited Options

The Vietnam e-visa system offers multiple processing timeframes to suit different travel schedules. Understanding these options helps you plan your application timing strategically.

### Standard Processing Timeline

**Standard processing takes 3–5 business days** from the date you submit your application. This timeline is reliable and consistent—our research found that most applications are approved within this window. However, "business days" means Monday through Friday, excluding Vietnamese public holidays. If you apply on a Friday afternoon, your application won't begin processing until Monday. During major Vietnamese holidays (Lunar New Year, National Day), processing may pause entirely, potentially extending timelines by several days.

For this reason, we recommend applying at least 7–10 days before your intended entry date to account for processing time plus any unexpected delays. This buffer also gives you time to address any issues if your application is rejected or requires additional information.

### Expedited Processing Options

If you're cutting it close, expedited processing is available through both the official portal and service providers. **Expedited processing typically takes 1–2 business days** and costs an additional fee (usually USD 10–20 on top of the visa fee). Some service providers offer same-day or next-day processing for an additional premium, though these ultra-fast options are significantly more expensive.

During our time researching visa timelines, we found that expedited processing is genuinely faster—not a marketing gimmick. However, it's worth noting that even expedited applications can face delays if there are issues with your documents or if the system experiences unusual volume. Never rely on same-day processing if you're arriving tomorrow; always build in at least one extra day as a safety margin.

> **Did You Know?** The Vietnam Immigration Department processes e-visa applications 24/7, but applications submitted during Vietnamese public holidays may [experience](https://getyourguide.tpo.lv/GuAFfGGK?subid=blog) delays of 1–3 additional days.

> *Source: [Vietnam Immigration Department Notice](https://evisa.gov.vn/)*

### Checking Your Application Status

You can check your e-visa status anytime using your application reference number and passport number on the official portal. The system shows real-time updates: "Processing," "Approved," or "Rejected." Most applicants receive approval notifications via email, but it's wise to check the portal directly rather than relying solely on email, as messages sometimes get caught in spam filters.

## 5. Common Rejection Reasons and How to Avoid Them

While the majority of e-visa applications are approved, some are rejected or require resubmission. Understanding the most common reasons helps you avoid costly delays.

### Document Quality Issues

The most frequent rejection reason is a poor-quality passport photo. The image must be:

- **Clear and in focus** (no blurry or pixelated photos)
- **Recent** (taken within six months)
- **Properly lit** (no shadows across your face)
- **Correct dimensions** (4×6 cm, which is standard passport photo size)
- **White or neutral background** (no colored or patterned backgrounds)

If your photo is rejected, you can resubmit with a new image without reapplying. Similarly, if your scanned passport page is too dark, blurry, or cut off, resubmit a clearer scan. Many rejections are simply due to document quality, not eligibility issues.

### Information Mismatches

Ensure every detail on your application matches your passport exactly:

- **Full name** must be spelled identically (including middle names and hyphens)
- **Passport number** must be correct to the last digit
- **Date of birth** must match your passport
- **Passport expiry date** must be at least six months away from your entry date

Even minor discrepancies—like writing "John" instead of "Jon" or transposing a digit in your passport number—result in rejection. Take time to verify these details before submitting.

### Entry Port Restrictions

Not all entry ports accept e-visa holders. While e-visas are accepted at 30+ major airports, seaports, and land borders, some smaller or remote entry points do not. When filling out your application, select your entry port carefully from the official list. If you're unsure where you'll enter (for example, if you're arriving via a cruise ship), contact the port authority or your cruise operator to confirm they accept e-visas before applying.

## 6. Eligible Entry and Exit Points Across Vietnam

One of the advantages of the e-visa is its acceptance at numerous entry points across Vietnam. However, not all borders accept e-visa holders, so choosing the right entry port is crucial for a smooth arrival.

### Major International Airports

The vast majority of international travelers enter Vietnam through one of its three major airports:

- **Noi Bai International Airport** (Hanoi) – accepts e-visas
- **Tan Son Nhat International Airport** (Ho Chi Minh City) – accepts e-visas
- **Da Nang International Airport** – accepts e-visas

These three airports handle the bulk of international [flights](https://trip.tpo.lv/TmObooZ5?subid=blog) and have the most efficient immigration processing. If you're planning to explore [North Vietnam](https://go2-vietnam.com/region/north-vietnam/) starting in [Hanoi](https://go2-vietnam.com/city/hanoi/), or heading to [South Vietnam](https://go2-vietnam.com/region/south-vietnam/) via [Ho Chi Minh City](https://go2-vietnam.com/city/ho-chi-minh-city/), these are your best options. [Da Nang](https://go2-vietnam.com/city/da-nang/) serves as a gateway to [Central Vietnam](https://go2-vietnam.com/region/central-vietnam/) and nearby destinations like [Hoi An](https://go2-vietnam.com/city/hoi-an/) and [Hue](https://go2-vietnam.com/city/hue/).

### Seaports and Cruise Terminals

If you're arriving by cruise ship, e-visas are accepted at major seaports including:

- **Hai Phong Port** (near [Ha Long Bay](https://go2-vietnam.com/city/ha-long/))
- **Ho Chi Minh City Port** (Saigon River)
- **Nha Trang Port**
- **Phu Quoc Port**

However, cruise operators vary in their policies—some arrange group visas, while others require passengers to have individual e-visas. Always confirm with your cruise line before booking.

### Land Border Crossings

E-visas are accepted at several major land borders, though not all. Popular crossings include:

- **Moc Bai** (Cambodia border, near Ho Chi Minh City)
- **Ha Tien** (Cambodia border, [Mekong Delta](https://go2-vietnam.com/region/mekong-delta/) region)
- **Lao Cai** (China border, near [Sapa](https://go2-vietnam.com/city/sapa/))

Before planning an overland entry, verify with the official e-visa portal that your chosen border crossing accepts e-visas. Some remote or newly opened borders may not yet be integrated into the system.

## 7. What to Do If Your E-Visa Is Rejected

Receiving a rejection notice is frustrating, but it's not the end of the road. Most rejections are fixable, and you can reapply quickly.

### Understanding the Rejection Reason

When your application is rejected, the portal provides a reason—usually one of these:

- **Document quality issue** – your photo or passport scan is unclear
- **Information mismatch** – details don't match your passport
- **Ineligible nationality** – your country isn't on the approved list (rare)
- **Invalid passport** – your passport is expired or expires too soon

Read the rejection notice carefully. If it's a document quality issue, simply resubmit with a clearer photo or scan. If it's an information mismatch, correct the details and reapply. You can submit a new application immediately—there's no waiting period between rejections.

### Reapplication Process

To reapply after rejection, return to the official portal and submit a new application. You'll pay the fee again, but this is the cost of correcting the error. If you've fixed the issue (e.g., obtained a better passport photo), your second application should be approved within the standard processing timeframe.

### Emergency Visa Options

If you're rejected and your travel date is imminent, you have backup options. You can apply for a traditional visa through a Vietnamese embassy or consulate, though this typically takes longer and costs more. Some travelers in urgent situations have obtained emergency visas through embassies in neighboring countries (Thailand, Cambodia, Laos) within 24 hours, though at a premium cost. This should be a last resort—plan ahead to avoid this scenario.

## 8. E-Visa Validity and Extension Options

Understanding how long your e-visa is valid and what happens if you need to stay longer is essential for trip planning.

### Validity Period and Duration of Stay

Your e-visa is valid for **90 days from your entry date into Vietnam**. This means if you enter on June 1st, your visa expires on August 30th. You can stay in Vietnam for the entire 90-day period, but you must exit before the expiration date. The 90-day period applies regardless of whether you have a single-entry or multiple-entry e-visa—the difference is how many times you can leave and re-enter during that window.

### Extending Your Stay

If you need to stay in Vietnam longer than 90 days, you have two options:

**Option 1: Extend before expiration** – Visit an immigration office in Vietnam (there are offices in all major cities including [Hanoi](https://go2-vietnam.com/city/hanoi/), [Ho Chi Minh City](https://go2-vietnam.com/city/ho-chi-minh-city/), and [Da Nang](https://go2-vietnam.com/city/da-nang/)) and apply for an extension. Extensions typically add 30 days and cost around USD 25–30. Processing takes 3–7 days.

**Option 2: Exit and re-enter** – Leave Vietnam before your 90 days expire and re-enter on a new e-visa. This works if you have a multiple-entry e-visa, or if you apply for a new single-entry e-visa for your return. Many long-term travelers use this approach, making brief trips to Cambodia or Thailand to reset their visa clock.

During our research into visa extensions, we found that immigration offices are generally efficient, but processing times vary by location and season. Apply well before your visa expires to avoid overstaying, which carries fines and potential deportation.

## 9. Comparing E-Visa to Other Vietnam Visa Options

While the e-visa is the most popular option for tourists, it's worth understanding how it compares to traditional visas and visa exemptions.

| Visa Type | Processing Time | Cost (USD) | Validity | Best For |
|-----------|-----------------|-----------|----------|----------|
| **E-Visa (Single Entry)** | 3–5 days | 25 | 90 days | Most international tourists |
| **E-Visa (Multiple Entry)** | 3–5 days | 50 | 90 days | Regional travelers, multiple trips |
| **Embassy Visa** | 5–10 days | 100–150 | 90 days | Those ineligible for e-visa |
| **Visa on Arrival** | 1–2 days | 25–50 | 90 days | [Last-minute](https://booking.tpo.lv/pDNjHJA1?subid=blog) travelers |
| **Visa Exemption** | None | Free | 30–90 days | Citizens of select countries |

The e-visa offers the best balance of cost, convenience, and processing time for most travelers. Embassy visas are more expensive and slower but may be necessary if your nationality isn't eligible for e-visas. Visa-on-arrival services (arranged through travel agents) are faster but typically more expensive than the official e-visa.

### Visa Exemptions

Citizens of certain countries enjoy visa-free entry to Vietnam for 30–90 days. These include some ASEAN nations and a few others. Check the official [Vietnam visa page](https://go2-vietnam.com/visa/) to see if your nationality qualifies. If you do qualify for visa-free entry and are staying under the allowed period, you can skip the e-visa entirely.

## 10. Pro Tips for a Smooth E-Visa Application and Entry

Based on extensive research and traveler feedback, here are practical tips to ensure your e-visa process goes smoothly:

### Before You Apply

**Passport validity** – Ensure your passport has at least six months validity remaining. Immigration officers are strict about this rule, and you'll be denied entry if your passport expires too soon, even with a valid e-visa.

**Photo preparation** – Take your passport photo in good natural light against a white or neutral wall. Use a smartphone camera if necessary—quality matters more than the device. Have the photo printed or saved digitally before you start your application.

**Information accuracy** – Gather your passport and write down your details before starting the online form. Rushing through the application is the leading cause of rejections.

**Backup documents** – Save copies of your e-visa confirmation email and reference number. Screenshot your approval notice as well. While immigration officials can verify your e-visa in their system, having digital copies prevents confusion.

### During Your Application

**Timing** – Apply 7–10 days before your entry date, even if standard processing is 3–5 days. This buffer accounts for processing delays and gives you time to address any issues.

**Browser compatibility** – The official portal works best on Chrome or Firefox. If you experience technical issues, try a different browser or device.

**Payment confirmation** – Keep your payment receipt. If your application isn't approved within the stated timeframe, you'll need this to contact support.

### At Immigration

**Presentation** – Have your passport ready and open to the information page. Immigration officers will scan your passport and verify your e-visa in their system. The process is quick—usually under two minutes.

**Honesty** – If asked about your purpose (tourism, business, etc.), answer truthfully. E-visas are for tourism and short-term business; if you're planning to work or stay long-term, you need a different visa type.

**Digital backup** – While not required, having a digital copy of your e-visa approval on your phone is helpful if there are any questions.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can I apply for a Vietnam e-visa if I'm already in Vietnam?

No. The e-visa must be applied for before you enter Vietnam. If you're already in the country and need to extend your stay, you must apply for an extension at a local immigration office, not an e-visa.

### What happens if I overstay my e-visa?

Overstaying a Vietnamese visa results in fines (typically USD 5–10 per day) and potential deportation. Your passport may be flagged, making future entry to Vietnam difficult. Always exit before your visa expires or apply for an extension in advance.

### Can I change my entry date after applying for an e-visa?

No. Once your e-visa is approved, your entry and exit dates are fixed. If your travel plans change, you must apply for a new e-visa with the updated dates. You cannot modify an existing approval.

### Is the e-visa accepted at all Vietnamese airports?

The e-visa is accepted at all major international airports (Hanoi, Ho Chi Minh City, Da Nang) and most secondary airports. However, some smaller regional airports may not have the system in place. Always verify your specific entry airport on the official portal before applying.

### Do I need a return ticket to apply for an e-visa?

No. The e-visa application doesn't require proof of a return ticket. However, immigration officers may ask to see your onward travel plans at the border, so it's wise to have a return flight booked or at least a plan for exiting Vietnam.

### Can I get a Vietnam e-visa for someone else?

Yes. You can apply on behalf of family members or friends by filling out their information on the application form and uploading their documents. However, you'll need their passport details and a digital copy of their passport photo.

### What if my passport is about to expire?
