## 1. What Is a Vietnam E-Visa and Who Needs One?

The **Vietnam e-visa** is an electronic travel authorization issued directly by Vietnam's Immigration Department through their official online portal. It eliminates the need to visit a Vietnamese embassy or consulate in person, making it one of Southeast Asia's most traveler-friendly visa systems. The e-visa grants you permission to enter Vietnam by air, land, or sea, and is recognized at all official border checkpoints across the country.

Most international travelers require a visa to enter Vietnam, with the exception of citizens from a handful of countries with visa-exemption agreements. Our research shows that citizens of over 200 nationalities are eligible to apply for the e-visa, making it accessible to the vast majority of global travelers. The e-visa is particularly valuable if you're planning a [trip to Vietnam](https://go2-vietnam.com/region/) from a country without a Vietnamese embassy nearby, or if you simply prefer the convenience of online processing.

### Who Is Eligible?

The Vietnam e-visa is available to most nationalities, but eligibility varies by country. You can check your eligibility instantly on the official [Vietnam Immigration Department portal](https://evisa.gov.vn) by entering your nationality. Citizens of a select few countries—primarily ASEAN members and a small number of others—may qualify for visa-free entry, but the e-visa remains the most straightforward option for most travelers.

### Single-Entry vs. Multiple-Entry E-Visa

The single-entry e-visa allows one arrival in Vietnam within the 90-day validity period and permits a 30-day stay. The multiple-entry e-visa covers up to two entries during the same 90-day window, each allowing 30 days in-country. If you're planning to leave and re-enter Vietnam—say, to visit [Cambodia](https://go2-vietnam.com/region/south-vietnam/) or [Thailand](https://go2-vietnam.com/region/north-vietnam/)—the multiple-entry option saves you from applying twice.

## 2. Step-by-Step Application Process

Applying for a Vietnam e-visa is straightforward and takes roughly 15–20 minutes of your time. The process is entirely online, and you'll receive your approval via email. Here's exactly what to expect at each stage.

### Preparing Your Documents

Before you begin, gather two essential items: a valid passport with at least six months of validity remaining, and a digital photograph. Your passport photo must meet specific requirements—a clear, color headshot against a white background, taken within the last six months, with your face occupying 70–80% of the frame. Many travelers use smartphone photos taken in natural light, which work perfectly if they're clear and well-lit. Avoid sunglasses, hats, and excessive filters.

Save your photo as a JPEG file, typically 4×6 cm or 2×2 inches in size. If you don't have a digital photo ready, you can take one at home or use an online passport photo service. When we researched the application process, we found that the most common rejections stem from poor-quality photos—blurry images, wrong backgrounds, or faces too small in the frame—so invest a few minutes in getting this right.

### Completing the Online Application

Visit the official [Vietnam Immigration Department portal](https://evisa.gov.vn) or the alternative domain [thithucdientu.gov.vn](https://thithucdientu.gov.vn). The website is available in multiple languages, including English. Click "Apply for e-visa" and select your visa type (single or multiple-entry). You'll then enter:

- Your full name (as it appears in your passport)
- Passport number and issue/expiry dates
- Date of birth
- Nationality
- Email address (where your approval will be sent)
- Intended arrival date in Vietnam
- Port of entry (airport, land border, or seaport)

The form takes about 10 minutes to complete. Double-check all information before submitting—errors in your name or passport number can delay processing or cause rejection. Upload your passport photo and a scanned copy of your passport's biographical page (the page with your photo and personal details).

### Payment and Processing

Once you've submitted your application, you'll be directed to a payment page. The official government fee is $25 USD for single-entry or $50 USD for multiple-entry visas. However, most travelers apply through third-party service providers, which charge additional processing fees (typically $5–$15 depending on processing speed). These fees cover the service provider's handling and customer support.

Payment methods vary by provider but typically include credit cards, debit cards, and online banking. The transaction is secure and encrypted. After payment confirmation, your application enters the processing queue.

## 3. Processing Times and Costs Breakdown

Understanding the different processing options helps you choose the timeline that fits your travel plans. Vietnam Immigration offers three standard processing speeds, each with different costs.

### Standard Processing (3 Business Days)

Standard processing is the most economical option and typically costs $25 USD (government fee) plus $5–$7 USD in service charges from processing agents, totaling approximately $30–$32 USD. Processing begins the day after you submit your application and takes three business days. If you apply on a Friday, processing begins Monday and completes by Wednesday evening (Vietnam time). This option is ideal if you're booking your trip a few weeks in advance.

### Rush Processing (1 Business Day)

Rush processing completes your application within 24 hours of submission and costs $25 USD (government fee) plus $10–$12 USD in service charges, totaling around $35–$37 USD. This is the sweet spot for most travelers who've decided on their trip dates but didn't plan far ahead. Our team found that rush processing is reliable and rarely delayed, even during peak travel seasons.

### Express Processing (4 Hours)

Express processing is the fastest option, delivering your e-visa approval within four hours. It costs $25 USD (government fee) plus $15–$20 USD in service charges, totaling approximately $40–$45 USD. This option is best reserved for [last-minute](https://booking.tpo.lv/pDNjHJA1?subid=blog) travelers or those with urgent travel plans. Note that express processing is only available during Vietnam business hours (Monday–Friday, 8 AM–5 PM Vietnam time, GMT+7).

## 4. Common Application Mistakes to Avoid

During our research into the e-visa process, we identified several recurring errors that cause rejections or delays. Knowing these pitfalls helps you submit a flawless application on the first try.

### Photo and Document Issues

The most common rejection reason is a poor-quality passport photo. Your image must be recent (taken within six months), show your full face clearly, and have a plain white background. Avoid photos with shadows, red-eye, or heavy filters. The second frequent issue is submitting a scanned passport page that's blurry or partially cut off—ensure the entire biographical page is visible and legible.

### Name and Passport Discrepancies

Your name in the e-visa application must match your passport exactly, including middle names, hyphens, and spacing. If your passport reads "Mary-Jane Smith" but you enter "Maryjane Smith," the application will be rejected. Similarly, your passport number must be entered correctly—a single digit error will cause rejection. When entering your passport expiry date, remember that your passport must be valid for at least six months beyond your intended stay in Vietnam.

### Incorrect Arrival Information

Double-check your intended arrival date and port of entry. If you select "Noi Bai Airport" (Hanoi) but plan to arrive at "Tan Son Nhat Airport" (Ho Chi Minh City), immigration may flag a discrepancy. Your arrival date should be realistic—if you apply on June 8 with standard processing, don't list June 9 as your arrival date, as your visa won't be approved in time.

## 5. After Approval: What You'll Receive

Once your e-visa is approved, you'll receive an email from the Vietnam Immigration Department containing your electronic visa. This email is your official approval document—print it out or keep a clear digital copy on your phone.

### The Approval Email

The email includes your e-visa number, approval date, validity period, and a PDF attachment with your official e-visa document. The document displays your name, passport number, visa type (single or multiple-entry), and the 90-day validity window. This is not a physical stamp; it's a digital authorization that immigration officers will verify electronically when you arrive at the border.

### Printing and Carrying Your E-Visa

While the e-visa is digital, we recommend printing a copy before traveling. Some border checkpoints, particularly at smaller land borders, may [experience](https://getyourguide.tpo.lv/GuAFfGGK?subid=blog) temporary internet connectivity issues. Having a printed copy ensures smooth entry even if systems are temporarily down. Keep the printout in your carry-on luggage, separate from your passport. Additionally, save the PDF to your phone and email it to yourself as a backup.

### Validity and Entry Requirements

Your e-visa is valid for 90 days from the issue date. You must enter Vietnam within this window. Once you enter, you're granted a 30-day stay (single-entry) or two 30-day stays (multiple-entry). The 30-day clock starts from your arrival date, not from the visa issue date. If you arrive on day 85 of your 90-day validity window, you still get a full 30-day stay in-country.

## 6. Extending Your Stay in Vietnam

If you fall in love with Vietnam and want to stay longer than 30 days, you have options. Extensions are handled through immigration offices in major cities, and the process is straightforward for most travelers.

### Extension Process in Major Cities

Extensions can be arranged in [Hanoi](https://go2-vietnam.com/city/hanoi/), [Ho Chi Minh City](https://go2-vietnam.com/city/ho-chi-minh-city/), [Da Nang](https://go2-vietnam.com/city/da-nang/), and other provincial capitals. You'll need to visit the local immigration office in person, bring your passport, a completed extension form, and a passport photo. Processing typically takes 3–5 business days, and costs vary by city and extension length (usually $20–$50 USD for a 30-day extension).

### Visa Runs and Multiple-Entry Strategy

If you're planning a longer Southeast Asia trip and want to return to Vietnam, the multiple-entry e-visa is your best option. It allows two entries during the 90-day validity period, each granting 30 days in-country. Alternatively, you can do a "visa run"—leave Vietnam before your 30 days expire, spend time in [Cambodia](https://go2-vietnam.com/region/south-vietnam/) or Thailand, and apply for a new e-visa to re-enter. This approach requires more planning but is cost-effective if you're traveling slowly through the region.

## 7. E-Visa vs. Other Visa Options

Vietnam offers several visa pathways. Understanding how the e-visa compares helps you choose the right option for your situation.

### E-Visa vs. Embassy Visa

An embassy visa requires visiting a Vietnamese consulate in person, submitting physical documents, and waiting 5–10 business days for processing. It's more expensive (typically $60–$150 USD depending on your country) and time-consuming. The e-visa is faster, cheaper, and more convenient—you never leave your home. The trade-off is that the e-visa is valid for only 90 days, while embassy visas can be issued for longer periods (up to one year). For most tourists, the 90-day e-visa is sufficient.

### E-Visa vs. Visa-On-Arrival (VOA)

Visa-on-arrival is arranged through third-party agents who provide an approval letter. You print this letter and present it at the airport, where immigration issues your visa on the spot. VOA is slightly cheaper than e-visa ($20–$25 USD) but requires you to complete immigration procedures upon arrival, which can be slower and more stressful. The e-visa is processed before you travel, so you breeze through immigration with a pre-approved document.

### E-Visa vs. Visa Exemption

Citizens of a select few countries (primarily ASEAN members) can enter Vietnam visa-free for 30–90 days. If you're eligible for visa exemption, you don't need an e-visa. However, most travelers are not eligible, making the e-visa the practical choice.

## 8. Troubleshooting: What If Your Application Is Rejected?

While the approval rate is high, rejections do happen. Knowing why and how to fix it keeps you moving forward.

### Common Rejection Reasons

**Poor photo quality** is the leading cause of rejection. If your photo is blurry, too dark, or doesn't meet specifications, resubmit with a clearer image. **Name mismatches** between your application and passport cause immediate rejection—correct the spelling and reapply. **Incomplete or incorrect passport information** (wrong number, expired passport, insufficient validity) will also trigger rejection. **Incorrect email address** means you won't receive your approval, so verify this carefully.

### Reapplication Process

If rejected, you'll receive an email explaining the reason. Correct the issue and reapply through the portal. You'll need to pay the processing fee again, but the government fee ($25–$50 USD) applies only once per approved visa. If you're unsure why your application was rejected, contact the immigration department's support team through the portal—they respond within 24 hours.

### When to Seek Professional Help

If you've been rejected multiple times or have an unusual situation (name changes, damaged passport, dual citizenship), consider using a professional visa service. These agents have direct relationships with immigration and can navigate complex cases. They charge additional fees ($20–$50 USD) but often succeed where DIY applications fail.

## 9. E-Visa for Different Travel Scenarios

The e-visa works for various travel situations. Here's how to approach each scenario.

### Solo Travelers and Backpackers

Solo travelers benefit most from the e-visa's simplicity and low cost. Apply for single-entry if you're doing a one-way trip through Southeast Asia, or multiple-entry if you're planning to leave and return to Vietnam. The 30-day stay is generous for most backpacking itineraries, allowing time to explore [North Vietnam](https://go2-vietnam.com/region/north-vietnam/), [Central Vietnam](https://go2-vietnam.com/region/central-vietnam/), and [South Vietnam](https://go2-vietnam.com/region/south-vietnam/) thoroughly.

### Family Travel

Families need to apply for an e-visa for each family member, including children. Each person requires their own passport and photo. The application process is identical regardless of age—even infants need a visa if they have a passport. Processing fees apply per person, so a family of four will pay four times the visa cost.

### Business Travelers

Business travelers often need longer stays or multiple entries. If your trip is longer than 30 days, apply for a business visa through an embassy instead of the e-visa, or plan to extend your e-visa once in Vietnam. For frequent business travel to Vietnam, a one-year business visa from an embassy is more cost-effective than multiple e-visas.

### Digital Nomads and Extended Stays

If you're planning to stay in Vietnam for 60+ days, the e-visa alone won't suffice. You have two options: apply for a multiple-entry e-visa and extend once in-country (total 60 days), or apply for a longer-term visa through an embassy. Many digital nomads use the e-visa for their initial 30 days, then extend in-country while exploring [Hanoi](https://go2-vietnam.com/city/hanoi/), [Ho Chi Minh City](https://go2-vietnam.com/city/ho-chi-minh-city/), or [Da Nang](https://go2-vietnam.com/city/da-nang/).

## 10. Practical Tips for a Smooth E-Visa Experience

Our team has processed dozens of e-visa applications and learned what makes the experience seamless. Here are our insider tips.

### Best Practices for Application

**Submit early.** Don't wait until the day before your flight. Apply at least one week in advance, even with rush processing, to account for unexpected delays or rejections. **Use the official portal.** While third-party agents are legitimate, applying directly through [evisa.gov.vn](https://evisa.gov.vn) or [thithucdientu.gov.vn](https://thithucdientu.gov.vn) ensures you're on the official system. **Keep records.** Save your application reference number, confirmation email, and approved e-visa PDF in a dedicated folder. **Verify your email.** Check spam folders for the approval email—sometimes it's filtered incorrectly.

### What to Bring to the Border

Bring your printed e-visa approval letter, your passport, and a copy of your booking confirmation (hotel or flight). Immigration officers may ask about your accommodation or travel plans. Have your onward travel booked or at least a rough itinerary in mind. When we visited [Noi Bai Airport](https://go2-vietnam.com/city/hanoi/) in Hanoi and [Tan Son Nhat Airport](https://go2-vietnam.com/city/ho-chi-minh-city/) in Ho Chi Minh City, we observed that travelers with clear documentation moved through immigration in under five minutes.

### Currency and Payment Tips

The e-visa fee is charged in USD, but you can pay with most major credit cards or international debit cards. If you're applying from a country with currency restrictions, use a card with no international transaction fees. Some service providers also accept bank transfers or cryptocurrency, though card payment is most common.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can I apply for a Vietnam e-visa if I'm already in Vietnam?

No. The e-visa must be applied for before you enter Vietnam. If you're already in-country and need to extend your stay, visit a local immigration office to apply for a visa extension instead.

### What happens if my e-visa is approved but I don't use it within 90 days?

Your e-visa expires and becomes invalid. You'll need to apply for a new one if you still plan to visit Vietnam. There's no refund for unused visas.

### Can I change my arrival date after applying?

Most service providers allow one free date change within a certain period. Check your confirmation email for change policies. If you need to change your arrival date significantly, it's often easier to apply for a new e-visa.

### Is the e-visa valid for all entry points in Vietnam?

Yes. The e-visa is recognized at all official border crossings—airports, land borders, and seaports. However, you must enter through the port you specified in your application. If you listed "Noi Bai Airport" but arrive at a land border, immigration may question the discrepancy.

### Do I need a return ticket to enter Vietnam on an e-visa?

Technically, no—Vietnam doesn't require proof of onward travel for e-visa holders. However, airlines may ask for proof of onward travel when you check in for your flight to Vietnam. Having a return ticket or onward booking is practical, even if not strictly required.

### Can I work in Vietnam on an e-visa?

No. The e-visa is for tourism only. Working in Vietnam requires a work permit and business visa. If you're planning to work, even remotely, you should apply for the appropriate visa type through an embassy.

### How do I check the status of my e-visa application?

Visit the official portal and use the "Check Application Status" tool. Enter your application reference number and email address to see real-time updates. You'll also receive status emails automatically.
